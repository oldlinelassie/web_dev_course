Records of the U.S. House of Representatives

The Records of the U.S. House of Representatives include a variety of record types, including bill files, bound volumes, committee papers, minute and docket books, and petitions and memorials. Records span from 1789 to present. They include material referred to and generated by the many committees, as well as records of the Clerk of the House of Representatives.

House records are divided into browse lists by type of committee (standing, select, or special) or administrative and support offices.

Standing Committees

Standing committees are permanently authorized by House rules.

Committee on Accounts (1805-1946)

Jurisdiction: All subjects touching the expenditure of the contingent fund of the House, auditing and settling of all accounts which may be charged therein to the House, accountability of officers of the House, procurement of rooms for the use of House committees and for the Speaker, recommending and authorizing the employment of such persons as stenographers, reporters of debates, janitors, and clerks and staff assistants for committees, members and senators.
